Project Overview

KRONOS is a passenger disruption recovery system developed as part of a senior Systems Analysis & Business Process Capstone. My team selected Delta Air Lines as the target organization and analyzed how passengers navigate delays, cancellations, refunds, vouchers, and customer support during operational disruptions.

After researching Delta's business environment and operational challenges, we designed KRONOS as a centralized decision-support platform that helps passengers understand their recovery options and take action more efficiently.

The Problem

Airline disruptions often create confusion for passengers. Travelers may need to compare rebooking options, request refunds, access travel credits, locate compensation benefits, or contact customer service — all while dealing with an unexpected interruption to their trip. These processes can become fragmented across multiple systems and communication channels, leading to longer resolution times and increased pressure on customer support teams.

The goal of KRONOS was to simplify the recovery experience by providing passengers with a single platform for managing disruption-related decisions.

Research & Analysis

The project began with a comprehensive business analysis of Delta Air Lines. Through structured research, we identified operational disruption recovery as the area where a technology-driven solution could deliver the most meaningful improvement to both customer experience and operational efficiency.

  • SWOT Analysis — Evaluated Delta's internal strengths and weaknesses alongside external market pressures affecting disruption handling.
  • Business Process Analysis — Mapped existing passenger recovery workflows to identify bottlenecks and fragmentation points.
  • Stakeholder Research — Identified key actors including passengers, gate agents, and customer support representatives and their respective needs.
  • Requirements Gathering — Translated research findings into functional and non-functional system requirements.
  • Workflow Evaluation — Assessed current-state processes to define the scope and boundaries of the proposed solution.

Proposed Solution

KRONOS is a centralized disruption recovery dashboard designed to help passengers make informed decisions during flight disruptions. By consolidating recovery actions into a single workflow, the platform aims to reduce passenger confusion while improving the overall resolution experience.

Smart Rebooking Assistance

Passengers can review and compare alternative flight options based on availability and travel preferences, reducing the time spent searching across separate channels.

Refund & eCredit Comparison

Provides clear, side-by-side information to help travelers evaluate refund requests versus future travel credits before committing to a resolution path.

Digital Compensation Management

Allows passengers to access and manage eligible vouchers and travel benefits in one place, without navigating separate airline systems.

Recovery Status Tracking

Provides real-time visibility into the progress of disruption-related requests and actions, keeping passengers informed throughout the resolution process.

Customer Support Escalation

Enables passengers to transfer relevant trip context directly to support representatives, reducing repeat explanations and accelerating issue resolution.

Unified Recovery Dashboard

A single interface consolidating all disruption-related options — the core architectural decision that differentiates KRONOS from Delta's current fragmented workflows.

System Design

KRONOS was documented using industry-standard systems analysis and design methodologies. Use case diagrams defined the interactions between passengers and the system, establishing core functionality and user requirements.
